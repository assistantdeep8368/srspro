Bituminous roads
Bituminous roads are made from a mixture of aggregates and bitumen (a by-product of crude oil).
Bitumen serves as a binding material, which is mixed with aggregates to form a strong surface.
View More

Interlocking roads
Interlocking roads are built using pavers made from materials
like concrete, which are highly durable and can withstand heavy traffic loads.
These roads are resistant to weather
changes, erosion, and general wear and tear, giving them a longer lifespan
compared to traditional asphalt or concrete roads.
